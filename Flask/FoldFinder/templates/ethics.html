The Internet & the Ethics of Origami

The free distribution of origami instructions via the Internet has opened up a host of interesting ethical and legal issues that are worth taking a moment to explore.


There are several parallels to be drawn between origami and music. Not only origami itself, but photos and other derivative media are all subject to copyright restrictions, much like musical recordings and scores are subject to copyright alongside musical compositions. If you want to use images of origami for commercial purposes, you have some permissions to obtain. Personal use is fine, though. More details are available at several different websites. You may also be interested in reading about this legal case decided in Japan in 2011.


In addition to these legalistic issues, there is a whole discourse concerning the ethical practice of origami itself. Are cuts OK? What about paper that isn't square? David Mitchell has a good summary of some of these and other issues, if you'd care to learn more.
